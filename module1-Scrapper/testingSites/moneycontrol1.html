      • April 15, 2024 / 04:01 PM IST

        Sensex Today | Jatin Gedia – Technical Research Analyst at Sharekhan by BNP Paribas

        Nifty opened gap down today and drifted lower during the course of the day to close with a deep cut of ~242 points. On the daily charts we can observe that the Nifty is in the process of retracing the rise it has witnessed from 22710 – 22776. The key 50 and 61.82% Fibonacci retracement levels are placed at 22240 – 22117. The daily momentum indicator has triggered a fresh negative crossover which is a sell signal. Thus, in case of a bounce towards 22350 – 22370 it should be used as a selling opportunity. On the upside, the gap area formed today in the range 22420 – 22500 shall act as an immediate hurdle from short term perspective.

        Bank Nifty also witnessed correction and closed down ~791 points. The next crucial Fibonacci support is placed at 47443 which also coincides with the 20-day moving average. Thus, 47400 – 47300 is the crucial support zone while 48250 – 48300 is the immediate hurdle zone from short term perspective. Minor degree bounce backs should be used as a selling opportunity.

      • April 15, 2024 / 04:00 PM IST

        Sensex Today | Kunal Shah, Senior Technical & Derivative Analyst at LKP Securities

        The Bank Nifty index has continued to face strong bearish pressure, with visible selling during intraday bounces. The index is poised to test its support at 47,500, which aligns with the 20-day moving average (20DMA). Failure to hold above this level could lead to further declines. Immediate resistance is established in the 48,500 to 48,600 zone. A decisive break above this resistance is necessary to resume the upward movement towards the 50,000 mark.

      • April 15, 2024 / 03:50 PM IST

        Sensex Today | Prashanth Tapse, Senior VP (Research), Mehta Equities

        The worsening situation in the Middle-East with conflict between Iran and Israel flaring up had engulfed Dalal Street. Investors worldwide, including in India, have turned cautious and resorted to profit-taking. If the situation turns bad, we may see extended broad-based selling in local markets. Geo political tensions and uncertainty over interest rate cut could have a bearing on the markets.

      • April 15, 2024 / 02:54 PM IST

        Sensex Today | Anuj Choudhary Research Analyst, Sharekhan by BNP Paribas

        Indian Rupee weakened on a strong US Dollar and a decline in domestic markets. Global risk sentiments deteriorated on renewed geopolitical tensions between Israel and Iran over the weekend. However, sharp downside in the Rupee was contained amid upbeat domestic macroeconomic data from India and possible intervention by the central bank. India’s CPI rose 4.85% in March 2024 versus 5.09% in February 2024. Industrial production increased by 5.7% in February 2024 versus 3.8% in January 2024. US Dollar rose to a 5-month high as Iran fired a barrage of missiles and armed drones on Israel targets.

        We expect Rupee to trade with a slight negative bias on heightened global tensions in the Middle East which could impact global equities and strengthen the Dollar further. Any further escalation of tensions may trigger fresh upside in crude oil prices which could further pressurise the Rupee. However, if the situation remains contained, we could see some recovery in the Rupee. Traders will take cues from India’s trade deficit and US retail sales data. USDINR spot price is expected to trade in a range of Rs 83.20 to Rs 83.80.
